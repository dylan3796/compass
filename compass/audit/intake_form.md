# Fleet Audit — Intake (kickoff call script)

45 minutes. Get answers in writing afterward — memories of this call diverge fast.

## The frame (2 min)

"In two weeks you get a report: every agent you run, what each costs, what's broken ranked in
dollars, and the agents you should build next with projected ROI. To get there I need data
access and about three hours of your team's time, total."

## Company

1. What does the company do, and which teams touch AI agents today?
2. Who owns AI/agents? (A person? A committee? Nobody? "Nobody" is a finding, not a problem.)
3. What's the monthly LLM spend, to the nearest order of magnitude? Who sees the bill?

## The fleet (as they believe it exists)

4. List every agent or LLM-powered workflow you know about. For each: what it does, who built
   it, what model, roughly how often it runs.
5. Which one do you trust the most? The least? Why?
6. What have individual teams wired up themselves that isn't on the platform team's list?
   (Ask this verbatim. Pause. Wait.)
7. Any agents that were turned off or abandoned? What happened?

## The gap

8. When you started with agents, what did you expect them to deliver? (Dollar figure, headcount
   equivalent, or "honestly, we were experimenting" — all fine.)
9. What do they actually deliver today, as far as you can tell?
10. If you could only know ONE number about your fleet, what would it be?

## Data reality

11. Where do agent logs live, and who can export them? (Name a person.)
12. Anthropic Console / OpenAI usage export access — who has it?
13. Are any agents built on Claude Code or the Claude Agent SDK? (If yes: they get the
    5-minute collector — best-instrumented part of the audit.)
14. Any compliance constraints on sharing logs/prompts? (Redaction is fine; silence is not.)

## What's next (their roadmap)

15. What agents are you planning or wishing for? What's blocked them?
16. Which team drowns in repetitive knowledge work the most?
