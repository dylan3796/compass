The problem

Agent fleets scale faster than your visibility into them.

01

Your agents are running. You just don't know how well.

Logs tell you an agent fired. They don't tell you whether it finished the job, how much it cost, or whether last week's version did it better.

02

Token spend ≠ output quality.

Most teams can't tell the difference. An agent reading an 80k-token document to produce a 10-line summary looks "busy" — it's actually your most expensive mistake.

03

Quality drops the day someone "improves" the prompt. Without a baseline, you find out weeks later — from a customer.

How it works

Connect → Define → Reconcile → Act.

Two MCP connections and an outcome definition. Then every Monday, every agent reports against the plan it was hired on.

STEP 1

Connect your agent via MCP

One config line. Claude Code, Codex, custom SDK agents — any harness that speaks MCP. Compass captures runs, token cost, latency, and completion signals.

STEP 2

Define the outcome in your systems

Point Compass at where the outcome already lives — Zendesk, Salesforce, HubSpot, Stripe — via the MCP connectors you already have. Pick a template (ticket deflection, pipeline created), set the unit value and the projection. Read-only, aggregates only.

STEP 3

Compass reconciles weekly

Delivered value is read from your system of record and compared against the plan. 147% or 49% — measured where your business keeps score, not self-reported by the agent.

STEP 4

One click to act

Fix the prompt, clone the winner, or add a guardrail. Then watch the value line — held, or dipped.
